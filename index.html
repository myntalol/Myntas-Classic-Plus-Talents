Creator's Message + General Patch Notes

Hi there! I created this site from scratch over 12 months following Season of Discovery. As an avid WoW player since 2009, Vanilla & TBC are my favorite eras of the game. Classic+ rumors inspired me to make my own class revamps that are grounded in an old school RPG vibe. While I love Vanilla, I believe any "Classic+" that might release should include class revamps, not just add content like new raids. I know some people disagree with that, and that's ok! Baldur's Gate 3 and Diablo 4 inspired me a lot during this time, so I incorporated ideas from those games and added Feats (which are similar to Runes in SoD).

TLDR: I made this to explore Classic+ ideas and wanted to share it with the community, feel free to let me know what you think!
- Mynta, GM of Aggrophobia, fear bombing mobs since 2006, Andorhal PvP | NA (@mynlol on Discord)

A "Classic+" Patch Notes of Sorts

  • Absorption effects such as Power Word: Shield no longer prevent resource generation or effect triggers when being hit (Ex. Rage and Righteous Fury)
  • Glaives, a new weapon type, has been added and is trainable as a skill for Warriors, Rogues and Hunters.
  • The Debuff limit has been removed on targets.
    Note - Old interviews with Classic WoW devs (like on ClassiCast) mention this being a computing limitation, not an intended power cap.
  • Pets now receive a percentage of their master's armor, spell damage, attack power, and stamina.
  • Periodic heals (HoTs) cast by different players can now be active on the same target simultaneously.
  • Legendary weapons have a new property: They no longer have durability.
  • World Buffs have had their overall effectiveness reduced.
    • Example: Rallying Cry of the Dragonslayer - Increases critical chance of spells by 5% (-5%), melee and ranged by 3% (-2%) and grants 50 attack power (-90).
    Note - World Buffs have an important place in any Classic WoW setting, but losing them to a wipe shouldn't make a player feel 20 levels weaker. Again, this is just my experiment with making their absence not feel as punishing.
  • Spirit now increases Spell Haste for all classes.
      Every 1 Spirit increases Spell Haste by 0.05%, which reduces the cast time of spells and the Global Cooldown. The Global Cooldown cannot be reduced below 1 sec. Does not affect melee or ranged attack speed.

      Formula:
      Hasted Cast Time = Base Cast Time / (1 + Spell Haste %)
    Note - Spirit has been updated to provide a small active combat benefit for spellcasters and healers. The conversion is strong enough to be felt but requires meaningful investment before it can significantly alter spell pacing. Spell Haste follows the Burning Crusade model set in place in the Sunwell patch.
  • Hero's Journey grants each class a unique quest at max level that results in a permanent upgrade.
    • (More details in the class pages.)
  • Arena Skirmishes are now available for players to queue up for in major cities.
    • Skirmishes grant a small amount of honor and allow you to hone your skills while waiting for a battleground.
  • New Dances are available for every race at level 60...after an extremely mysterious quest line of course!
  • Barbershops are available in every major city. (Hairstyles, hair color, markings, horns, and jewelry only.)
  • Armor Dyeing Recipes are now available for each respective Profession.
    • Example: Crimson Steel Enamel - Changes the hue of a Plate armor item. Requires a Blacksmith to apply.
    Note - Baldur's Gate 3 did an excellent job with their dye system. In Classic+, I would want to see a similar approach. No transmog should exist in Classic WoW, but a system where each Profession handles dyeing armor, opening up new economic avenues for crafters while keeping the visual cues from the items you wear intact would be great.

For class revamps and balance changes, head to the talent trees...thanks for exploring!
